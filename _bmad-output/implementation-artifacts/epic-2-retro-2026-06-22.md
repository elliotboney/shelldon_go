## What worked (keep doing)

1. **synctest discipline held across the whole epic.** Every time-based behavior — blink jitter/idle, mood-drift cadence, scheduler independent cadences, arbiter timeout — is a deterministic fake-clock test (AD-10). Zero flaky time tests.
2. **Early seams paid off as designed.** 2.3/2.4 were deliberately shaped as thin `Serve` loops so 2.5 could absorb them into one tier-shaped scheduler with no rewrite; 2.6's timeout slotted into the arbiter's existing reject path. The turn tier (3.5) is pre-wired (Yui's condition).
3. **Code review caught real, shipping-grade bugs both rounds.** 2.5: a `minDelay` floor that pre-empts a 3.5 computed-cadence busy-loop. 2.6: a worker-panic token-leak that would have frozen the pet in production. The different-model review step is earning its cost — keep it.
4. **`recover()`-per-goroutine (AD-5) is now reflexive.** Applied to scheduler jobs (2.5) and arbiter turns (2.6) consistently — "recover does not cross goroutines" is internalized.
5. **Import hygiene held.** depguard + imports-tests kept `core/` LLM-free and the reflex tier worker-free throughout.

## Recurring patterns (from deferred-work.md, 13 stories)

1. **`hub.Publish` blocking-send back-pressure — deferred 5×** (1.2, 1.5, 2.2, 2.3, 2.6). A blocking send with no context-aware escape; if a consumer stops draining, the producer hangs. Accepted at M0 as "16-slot buffer + draining consumer." **This is the dominant debt theme**, and Epic 3 stresses it directly: Telegram (3.4) is a network transport that can stall, and the real worker (3.3) is slow.
2. **Silent `Publish` error discard** (1.5, 2.6). `_ = hub.Publish(...)` drops `ErrNoRoute` with no log — blocked on AD-17 slog not yet being wired.
3. **Context-cancellation gaps, closing incrementally** (1.3 → fixed 2.6; non-cooperative worker leak 2.6; readLoop not stoppable 1.5). The turn lifecycle (AD-11) is being built story by story. **Hard requirement carried into 3.3:** the real worker must honor `ctx` cancellation or the timeout-abandon path leaks goroutines.
4. **Tests with no per-test deadline** (1.4, 2.3, 2.6). Unbounded blocking receives hang instead of failing cleanly; `go test -timeout` is the only net.
5. **Type-safety / closed-set gaps** (1.1 AllKinds, 1.2 empty Kind, 2.2 RegionID not structurally closed). Not urgent for Epic 3; relevant before Epic 6 plugin region-claims (AD-14).
