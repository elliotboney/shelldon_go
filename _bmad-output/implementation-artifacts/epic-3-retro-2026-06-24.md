## What worked (keep doing)

1. **Pre-built seams cashed out exactly as Epic 2 predicted.** 3.3's real worker dropped into the unchanged `Worker` seam (AD-2) that the Epic 1 stub defined. 3.5's turn tier layered onto 2.5's scheduler with a **literal zero diff to `scheduler.go`** (Yui's condition, verified by `git status`). 3.6 reused 3.5 via a single additive `OnResult` field — no rewrite.
2. **Narrow-interface + fake test-double pattern, applied 4×** — `broker.Provider`, `monolith.Completer`, `telegram.Client`, `turntier.Submitter`. Every edge is testable without its real backend; 32 new tests, zero real credit burned, zero network.
3. **Layering fences held transitively, not just directly.** The hard call in 3.3 — putting the broker-importing worker impl in `worker/monolith` (imported only by `main`) rather than `worker` — kept `go-openai` out of `core/`'s transitive graph. `telego` likewise confined to `transport/`. Proven by `core/dispatch/imports_test.go` staying green.
4. **synctest discipline carried from Epic 2** into the turn tier and proactive tests — daily-budget reset across a 25h fake span, cooldown suppression, cadence independence — all deterministic.
5. **Different-model code review kept earning its cost** — each story produced concrete, shipping-grade deferred-work entries (budget-slot-on-failure, test-channel-4×-prod, Telegram degraded crash-loop), not rubber stamps.

## Recurring debt themes (from deferred-work.md)

1. **Blocking `hub.Publish` back-pressure — the dominant theme, still open.** Present in dispatch, proactive, telegram, compositor. M1 safety net is the 16-slot buffer + draining consumer. **Being fixed now** (AI-1).
2. **Telegram degraded-transport supervisor crash-loop** (new, 3.6 review) — `main.go` sets the degraded `Serve` to return its init error immediately; suture restarts it in a tight loop with no meaningful backoff. **Being fixed now** (AI-2).
3. **Lossy budget accounting** — `turntier.tryConsume` spends a daily slot before `Submit`, so a transient provider failure burns budget with no ping. By-design for M1; revisit with durable/rollback budgets in Epic 4 (AI-4).
4. **`ErrNoRoute` silently discarded** on `_ = hub.Publish(...)` across core — low-priority observability gap (AI-3).
5. **Validation gaps at the broker/worker boundary** (empty Input, empty Messages/model, empty resp.Text) — defer to Epic 4 when input assembly is formalized.
